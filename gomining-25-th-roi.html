Two things get bought here, and most calculators only count the first. You mint 25 TH for about $509, and to hold the maximum 20% fee discount you must lock roughly $210 of GMT (360 days of fee coverage) — $719 of capital committed in total. On the income side, the hashrate nets about $8/month after fees and the 2% conversion, and the locked GMT earns roughly $4/month staking at 23.1% APR — about $12 combined. On total capital, that is a long payback at a flat price, so the case rests largely on Bitcoin appreciating from here.

The flat-price break-even is the pessimistic case. Bitcoin has never held one price across a halving cycle. Holding it flat, this setup breaks even in about 7.8 years. If Bitcoin instead follows published analyst forecasts, break-even shortens to around 2.1 years Forecast path: Standard Chartered ($500k by 2028), Bernstein ($1M by 2033), interpolated from today's price, with mining rewards still eroded by halvings and rising difficulty. A scenario, not a promise — Bitcoin could also fall.
Or reinvest instead of cashing out. Break-even assumes you pocket the rewards. Feed them back in weekly — buying 12 W hashrate and topping up GMT to hold the 20% discount, the way the optimizer allocates capital — and the farm compounds. On the analyst-forecast price path it grows to ~132 TH earning ~$229/mo in 3 years Floor case: at a flat Bitcoin price it holds ~37.7 TH earning ~$11/month — rising difficulty caps per-TH income, so growth mostly offsets decay. Compounding at 23.1% GMT staking plus reinvested mining. A growth path, not a promise.

Why the locked GMT is not a normal cost

Unlike the hashrate, the $210 in GMT isn't spent — you still own the tokens and can unlock them later, so it's capital tied up rather than money gone (with GMT price risk while it's locked). It also pulls double duty: it cuts your fee by 20% and earns 23.1% staking. Without any discount, 25 TH nets only about $4/month and ties up no GMT — but you leave the fee saving and the staking on the table. That trade is the main lever you control.

What the projection assumes

Break-even holds Bitcoin and GMT flat (BTC at $61,856), erodes mining rewards over time through halvings and rising network difficulty, and keeps staking income steady.
