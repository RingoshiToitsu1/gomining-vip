As of July 5, 2026, Bitcoin trades near $63,412 and the network mines about 47 sats per TH per day. On those numbers a 100 TH GoMining farm nets roughly $33/month from mining, and the $839 of GMT locked for the 20% discount adds about $16/month in staking — around $49 combined, for a break-even of about 7.5 years on $2,802 of committed capital. In short, that is a long payback at a flat price, so the case rests largely on Bitcoin appreciating from here.

The flat-price break-even is the pessimistic case. Bitcoin has never held one price across a halving cycle. Holding it flat, this setup breaks even in about 7.5 years. What would change this

These figures move constantly. A rising Bitcoin price improves margin (fees are fixed in dollars); rising difficulty erodes sats per TH; and letting your GMT coverage lapse can wipe out the discount and gut your net.
